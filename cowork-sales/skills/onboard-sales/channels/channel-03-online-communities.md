# Channel 03 — Online communities (FB groups / Discord / Skool / Reddit)

## What it means
You participate genuinely in existing communities where your target buyers already gather — building authority through helpfulness before ever mentioning an offer.

## Daily activity gist
Post 1 original thread in 1-2 communities + answer 3-5 questions with real depth + DM anyone who engages with your posts

## Suggested tactics (auto-selected by /onboard-sales Phase 3)
1. Post one original "teach something useful" thread per community per day — no promotion, pure value
2. Find 3-5 questions in the community feed and write the most thorough, useful answer in the thread
3. Send a friendly DM to anyone who liked, commented on, or directly thanked one of your posts — continue the conversation; never pitch on first contact

## Sample outbound prompt (rotated in /daily-sales-ritual)
> "Write a community post for [FB group / Skool / Discord] — one practical tip about [topic] that members can act on today — conversational tone, 150-200 words, no links."

## Buyer-pyramid signal patterns (Tool 4.2)
- **Buying now:** DM'd you asking "do you offer this as a service?" or "where can I buy your thing?"; tagged you in a post asking for a recommendation
- **Open:** Replied to your thread with a follow-up question; liked multiple of your answers this week; followed you outside the group
- **Interested:** Upvoted or reacted to your posts consistently; mentioned your advice to someone else in the thread
- **Quiet but listening:** Viewed your profile in the community; never interacts but you see them in member lists

## When this channel is wrong for the user
Your target audience doesn't actively participate in online communities — the channel only works where buyers are already present and engaged.
