# Profile 07 — New solopreneur

## Who this fits
You're early — no audience, no list, no established network. You might have an offer idea or a skill to sell, but you haven't proven it in the market yet. The goal is to generate your first 10 paying customers as efficiently as possible. Examples: freelancer just going independent, employee launching a side business, someone who just finished building their first product and hasn't sold anything yet.

## Smart-suggestion output (Phase 2 of /onboard-sales)

## Why these channels
Online communities are the fastest zero-cost shortcut to an audience you don't have yet — your buyers are already gathered in Facebook groups, Skool communities, Reddit forums, or Discord servers. Showing up with genuine helpfulness builds authority and relationships faster than building your own platform from scratch.

Direct cold outreach is the most controllable way to generate your first revenue — you choose who to reach out to, you control the message, and you can start today with zero followers or email list. The first 10 customers almost always come from direct conversations, not inbound traffic.

## Tactic seed (Phase 3 auto-fill)
- Channel #03 → Join 2-3 communities where your target buyer is active; answer 3-5 questions per day with genuine depth; DM anyone who engages with your answer
- Channel #05 → Build a list of 50 specific people who fit your ideal buyer profile; send 10 personalized first-touch messages per day; follow up once at day 4

## Common offer shape
New solopreneurs should start with the simplest offer possible — a single service, done-for-you or done-with-you, at a price that removes all buyer friction ($200-$1,500). The goal is proof: a paying customer, a testimonial, and a repeatable conversation that leads to a sale. Do not build a course or a funnel until you've sold something manually first.

## Dream 100 default
No — Dream 100 requires existing social capital to give before you ask. Build your first channel and your first customers before activating this lever.
