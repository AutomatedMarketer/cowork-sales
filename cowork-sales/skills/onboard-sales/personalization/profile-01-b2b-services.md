# Profile 01 — B2B services

## Who this fits
You sell services or software to other businesses — agencies selling to brands, consultants selling to executives, SaaS products selling to teams. Your buyer is a professional making a business decision, not a personal one. Examples: marketing agency, fractional CMO, B2B SaaS, IT consulting.

## Smart-suggestion output (Phase 2 of /onboard-sales)

> "Based on your business-brain, I see you're a B2B service provider. For someone in your shape, I'd suggest **Channel #02 (Social presence)** + **Channel #01 (Owned email list)**. But here are all 8 — pick 1-2 PRIMARY for YOU."

## Why these channels
LinkedIn and social content are the primary trust-building mechanism for B2B buyers — your prospect is scrolling LinkedIn during their workday, and a consistent presence there converts strangers into warm leads before you ever have a conversation. Sharing frameworks, case studies, and contrarian takes attracts the exact buyers who can make a purchase decision.

Your email list becomes the conversion layer — once someone follows you socially, the list is where the real relationship deepens. B2B buyers need more touches and more proof before committing, and email gives you the space to deliver both.

## Tactic seed (Phase 3 auto-fill)
- Channel #02 → Post one LinkedIn insight or mini case study per day (150-300 words); comment on 5-10 posts from decision-makers in your target accounts
- Channel #01 → Send one weekly email with a depth-first breakdown of a problem your buyers face; include one soft CTA to a discovery call or case study

## Common offer shape
B2B service providers typically sell a high-ticket engagement ($2,000-$20,000+) that solves a specific, measurable business problem — lead generation, operational efficiency, revenue growth. The offer is often framed as a retainer, project, or "done-for-you" service with clear deliverables and an ROI story.

## Dream 100 default
No — most B2B service providers can generate consistent revenue through their own social presence and list before needing strategic partnerships. Build the channel first.
