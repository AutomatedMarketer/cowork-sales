# Profile 04 — Creator / influencer

## Who this fits
Your primary asset is an audience you've built around your personality, expertise, or content — and you monetize that audience through sponsorships, products, or services. Examples: YouTube creator with a course, Instagram influencer with brand deals, newsletter writer with paid tiers, podcast host with a mastermind, TikTok creator launching a digital product.

## Smart-suggestion output (Phase 2 of /onboard-sales)
The wizard says:
> "Based on your business-brain, I see you're a creator building an audience-based business. For someone in your shape, I'd suggest **Channel #02 (Social presence)** + **Channel #07 (Borrowed audience)**. But here are all 8 — pick 1-2 PRIMARY for YOU."

## Why these channels
Social is your primary channel — it's not optional, it's the business. Consistent, high-quality content on your primary platform is how you attract followers, deepen trust, and generate inbound purchase intent. Your content IS your marketing.

Borrowed audience accelerates reach — guest appearances on other creators' platforms (podcast swaps, YouTube collabs, Instagram Lives, newsletter recommendations) expose you to pre-qualified audiences who already trust someone in your space. One well-placed collab can add thousands of followers in a week, faster than any other organic method.

## Tactic seed (Phase 3 auto-fill)
- Channel #02 → Post one piece of signature content per day on your primary platform; respond to every comment for the first 60 minutes after posting to signal the algorithm and deepen relationships
- Channel #07 → Send one "give" action per week to a fellow creator (share their content, leave a review, send a referral); make one collab ask per month to a creator whose audience overlaps with yours

## Common offer shape
Creators monetize through a range of structures depending on their stage: brand sponsorships (flat fee per post/episode), digital products (templates, presets, ebooks at $27-$197), premium memberships or communities ($29-$99/month), and high-ticket programs (workshops, masterminds, courses at $500-$5,000).

## Dream 100 default
Yes — creators are the natural category for borrowed-audience growth. Collaboration is table stakes in the creator economy.
