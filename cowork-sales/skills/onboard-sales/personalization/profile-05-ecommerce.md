# Profile 05 — E-commerce

## Who this fits
You sell physical or digital products through an online store — either branded products you own, dropshipped products, or print-on-demand. Examples: DTC supplement brand, clothing brand on Shopify, Amazon FBA seller, print-on-demand store, artisan product brand, beauty or wellness product line.

## Why these channels
Paid ads are the primary acquisition engine for most e-commerce brands — Facebook and Instagram ads in particular allow precise targeting by interest, demographic, and behavior, which matches your need to reach buyers who've never heard of you. With a working product and a tested offer, paid ads provide a scalable, repeatable growth lever.

Your email list is the profit center — in e-commerce, repeat buyers are 5x more profitable than first-time buyers. An email list lets you run post-purchase sequences, upsells, abandoned-cart recovery, VIP campaigns, and new product launches to buyers who've already converted once. The combination of paid ads for acquisition and email for retention is the DTC growth stack.

## Tactic seed (Phase 3 auto-fill)
- Channel #08 → Check campaign metrics every morning (CTR, CPP, ROAS); rotate creative weekly; run one retargeting campaign to cart abandoners and page viewers
- Channel #01 → Automate a post-purchase email sequence (thank you → story → upsell → review request) and a weekly broadcast highlighting new products or customer stories

## Common offer shape
E-commerce offers are product-centric with strong visual hooks, social proof (reviews, UGC), and a clear value proposition around quality, uniqueness, or price. Common tactics: bundle pricing, subscription/refill models, limited editions, free-shipping thresholds, and loyalty programs.

## Dream 100 default
No — e-commerce brands typically scale through paid acquisition and retention before needing strategic partnership plays.
