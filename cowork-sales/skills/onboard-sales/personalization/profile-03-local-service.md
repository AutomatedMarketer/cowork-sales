# Profile 03 — Local service

## Who this fits
You serve customers in a specific geographic area — your buyers live, work, or operate near you. Examples: local marketing agency, real estate agent, restaurant, dental practice, home services contractor, personal trainer with a local studio, local accounting firm.

## Why these channels
Local relationships are the most durable sales channel for a geographically constrained business. Chamber of Commerce mixers, BNI chapters, local masterminds, community events, and your own hosted workshops put you in front of people who can refer, hire, or partner with you. The in-person trust loop is faster than any digital channel when your buyers are in the same city.

Search and content is the compounding layer — local SEO (Google Business Profile, "best [service] in [city]" content) drives discovery from buyers who are actively looking. A single well-optimized blog post or YouTube video can generate inbound leads for years.

## Tactic seed (Phase 3 auto-fill)
- Channel #06 → Attend one local networking event this week; collect 5 contacts; send a personal follow-up within 48 hours to each
- Channel #04 → Publish one "how to [solve local problem] in [city]" blog post or YouTube video this week; optimize your Google Business Profile with a fresh photo and a response to every recent review

## Common offer shape
Local service providers sell recurring or project-based services with a geographic constraint — monthly retainers for ongoing services, one-time project fees, or per-appointment pricing. Offers are often positioned around speed ("available this week"), proximity ("serving [city]"), and trust ("5-star rated locally").

## Dream 100 default
No — local service businesses grow fastest through direct relationships and local visibility before they need strategic national partnerships.
