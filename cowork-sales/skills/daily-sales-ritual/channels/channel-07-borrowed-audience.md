# Channel 07 — Borrowed audience (podcast guesting / JV / Top 100)

## What it means
You get in front of other people's audiences — as a podcast guest, webinar co-host, newsletter swap, or joint-venture partner — borrowing trust the host has already built with their community.

## Daily activity gist
Make 1 "give" contribution per week to a target relationship + send 1 ask per quarter to borrow their audience

## Suggested tactics (auto-selected by /onboard-sales Phase 3)
1. Identify your top 100 peers, influencers, and list-owners in your space and categorize them into Give/Ask tiers; focus entirely on giving before asking
2. This week: do one public, visible act of value for a "Give" target — share their content, leave a meaningful public testimonial, send a referral, or send a resource they'd find useful
3. Once every 90 days per relationship: make one specific ask — guest on their podcast, co-host a webinar, do a newsletter swap, or offer a free training to their audience

## Sample outbound prompt (rotated in /daily-sales-ritual)
> "Write a warm outreach message to [name], a [type of person] with [audience size] in [niche]. I want to [podcast guest / newsletter swap / co-host a webinar]. Reference the give I've already done for them and make one specific ask. Max 6 sentences."

## Buyer-pyramid signal patterns (Tool 4.2)
- **Buying now:** A listener or subscriber from someone else's audience DM'd or emailed you directly asking about your offer after you appeared on their platform
- **Open:** Someone from a borrowed audience opted into your list or followed you immediately after a guest appearance
- **Interested:** Engaged with the episode or content (review, comment, share) and connected on social but hasn't reached out directly
- **Quiet but listening:** Followed you after the appearance but shows no further activity

## When this channel is wrong for the user
You don't have 5+ genuine peer relationships yet — borrowed audience requires social capital that must be built first.
