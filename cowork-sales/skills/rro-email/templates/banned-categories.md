# Banned RRO categories — hard-blocked

If the user's Q2 (the RRO catalyst) matches ANY of these patterns, the skill REJECTS the draft request and shows the 6 allowed categories instead.

## The 6 BANNED patterns

1. ❌ "Going back in the vault" / "Re-opening" / "Bringing back" — implies fake scarcity
2. ❌ "I'm excited to share..." — no real catalyst, just enthusiasm
3. ❌ "Haven't emailed in a while" / "It's been too long" — guilt, not news
4. ❌ "Seasonal sale" / "Holiday special" (without operational reason) — manufactured urgency
5. ❌ "We're launching" / "Opening doors" (without time-bound real constraint) — vague urgency
6. ❌ "Don't miss out" / "Last chance" (without real deadline) — fake scarcity

## The 6 ALLOWED categories

1. ✅ **Market Shift** — something changed in your industry. Be specific: what changed, when, why it matters.
2. ✅ **Client Result / New Data** — a real outcome from a real client. Name them (with permission) or describe specifics.
3. ✅ **Mistake or Lesson** — a public mistake you made, what you learned, why it matters to them.
4. ✅ **Real Deadline / Capacity** — operational constraint with a true cause + date (e.g., "I'm closing the doors on Friday because I'm onboarding 4 new clients next week").
5. ✅ **Controversy / Myth to Correct** — a common belief in your space that's costing your audience.
6. ✅ **New Access / New Resource** — something genuinely new the buyer can act on (new offer, new tool, new partnership).

## What the skill does when banned RRO detected

```
The skill says:

"That sounds like <banned category>. Real outreach needs a real reason. Here are the 6 categories that pass the skeptic test:

1. Market Shift
2. Client Result / New Data
3. Mistake or Lesson
4. Real Deadline / Capacity
5. Controversy / Myth to Correct
6. New Access / New Resource

Which one fits what's actually true today?"
```
