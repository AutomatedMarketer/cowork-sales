# Sequence Variant A: 5-Day Nurture

Use this when the audience is warm but not yet buying. The sequence opens a crisis, sustains tension across days, and closes with a stacked hard offer.

## Structure overview

| Day | Function | Ends with |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Hook / Crisis | Open loop (cliffhanger — do NOT resolve) |
| 2 | Drama / Struggle | Tease the discovery |
| 3 | Secret Reveal | Link to offer |
| 4 | Proof | 3+ client results with names and numbers |
| 5 | Hard Offer | Stack + guarantee + real deadline |

## Sequencing rules

- Send Day 1 at 8am local. Days 2-5 at the same time each day.
- Subject lines: connected across the series — Day 2 should reference Day 1.
- If a subscriber opens Day 1 but doesn't click Day 3's offer link → tag them and send a "Day 3 follow-up" 24 hours later with a different angle on the same proof.
- If a subscriber clicks but doesn't buy after Day 5 → move to a 3-day deadline extension sequence (not covered here — that's a separate sequence).

## Day 1 template: Hook / Crisis

```
Subject: <Name the specific crisis or mistake>

[Line 1-2: Drop into the middle of the crisis — present tense if possible]

[Line 3: The specific moment it broke — date, what happened, the exact number]
[Line 4: What you lost / cost / consequence — make it real]
[Line 5: What you didn't understand yet — hint at the lesson without giving it]

[Line 6-7: Their parallel reality — "If you've ever felt X, this is why"]
[Line 8: Why you're telling them this now — the RRO]

[Line 9: The thing you discovered that changed everything — one sentence tease]
[Line 10: Why you can't share the full thing today — force them to open tomorrow]

[Line 11: Open loop close — "Tomorrow I'll show you the exact step that turned it around"]

Reply: [anything — just want to know you read this]
```

## Day 1 example

```
Subject: I lost the client in 47 seconds

I was on a discovery call in March when the prospect asked one question.
I answered it wrong.

The call had started at 10:04am.
By 10:51, I had a "thanks but no thanks" reply in my inbox.
Net revenue: $0. Time spent on the lead: 11 hours.

I'd been getting that question wrong for two years and never knew it.

If you've ever left a sales call thinking "I thought that went well" and
then heard nothing — you know the specific silence I mean.

This week I'm walking through the 4 call mistakes I made in Q1 and
exactly what I replaced them with.

Today: the one question that was costing me closes.
Tomorrow: what I said instead — and the first call I used it on.

Reply with "I want to know" and I'll send you tomorrow's email first.
```

## Day 2 template: Drama / Struggle

```
Subject: <Reference yesterday — advance the story>

[Line 1-2: Recap one line — the stakes from Day 1]
[Line 3: The struggle period — how long, what you tried, what failed]
[Line 4-5: Specific failed attempts — name them with dates or numbers]
[Line 6: The turning point moment — what you finally saw differently]

[Line 7-8: Why most people miss this — the common pattern that hides it]
[Line 9: The thing you found — name it without explaining it yet]

[Line 10: Bridge to their situation — "You're probably doing the same thing"]
[Line 11: Tomorrow's reveal — what you'll share and why it matters]

[Line 12: Soft CTA — reply or stay tuned]
```

## Day 3 template: Secret Reveal + Offer Link

```
Subject: <Name the secret directly>

[Line 1-2: The reveal — no more teasing, state it plainly]
[Line 3-5: The specific mechanics — how it works, why it works]
[Line 6: Result it produced for you — number + timeframe]

[Line 7-8: How to apply it — brief actionable frame]
[Line 9: The faster path — mention the offer once, plainly]

[Line 10: Social proof bridge — "Three people used this in April"]
[Line 11: Offer link with one-sentence explanation]
[Line 12: Soft deadline — "I'm closing this page Friday at 5pm EST"]

[Link: <offer URL>]
```

## Day 4 template: Proof

```
Subject: <Client name> did <specific result>

[Line 1-2: Client 1 — name, result, timeframe]
[Line 3: The specific action they took — not vague effort, a single decision]

[Line 4-5: Client 2 — name, result, timeframe]
[Line 6: What made their result different from average]

[Line 7-8: Client 3 — name, result, timeframe — include the before state]
[Line 9: The common thread across all 3]

[Line 10: Why you're sharing these today — RRO callback]
[Line 11: Restate offer with deadline — shorter, harder]
[Line 12: CTA — one link, one action]
```

## Day 5 template: Hard Offer (Stack + Guarantee + Deadline)

```
Subject: Closes tonight at <time>

[Line 1-2: State the deadline plainly — no drama, just the fact]
[Line 3: What they get — stack list, specific items with names not vague descriptors]
[Line 4: The main result it's built for]
[Line 5: Guarantee — specific and unconditional if possible]

[Line 6-7: What happens if they don't act — honest consequence framing]
[Line 8: Remove the main objection — one sentence, direct]

[Line 9: The CTA — Buy or Apply, one link]
[Line 10: Deadline restate — date + time + timezone]
[Line 11: P.S. — the most important proof point you haven't said yet]
```
