# 12-line email skeleton (the universal format)

Every single email follows this shape — exactly 12 lines + 1 CTA.

```
Line 1:  <The catalyst, sentence 1>
Line 2:  <The catalyst, sentence 2 — sharp opener>

Line 3:  <The specific thing — what happened, when, who>
Line 4:  <Proof point 1 — date, number, name>
Line 5:  <Proof point 2 — date, number, name>
Line 6:  <Proof point 3 — date, number, name>

Line 7:  <Why this matters to THEM — bridge>
Line 8:  <Connection to their reality — sentence 1>
Line 9:  <Connection to their reality — sentence 2>

Line 10: <What you're going to do because of it>
Line 11: <The CTA setup — one sentence>
Line 12: <The CTA itself — Buy OR Apply OR Reply>
```

## Rules
- One CTA. Pick one of: Buy, Apply, Reply.
- Proof density (lines 4-6) lives or dies the email. Vague claims = weak email.
- Lines 7-9 must bridge to the reader's specific reality — never generic "this might help you."
- The catalyst (lines 1-2) must pass the Readiness Test Q3 (would a skeptic accept it?).
- Total length: 150-250 words. Brevity signals confidence.

## Strong example (proof density, single CTA, real RRO)

```
Last Tuesday Tracy posted on LinkedIn for the first time using the
podcast pitching micro-plan.

She'd never proactively pitched a podcast before in her career.

By end of day Tuesday, 2 interviews booked.
Year goal: 10 interviews.
She's at 20% from one post.

You told me last month you wanted to be on 5 podcasts this year and
hadn't pitched any. I'm running the same micro-plan with 4 other
people this month — same single LinkedIn post format.

I'm opening 6 spots this week.
Reply with "in" and I'll send you the post template + your first 3
podcast targets within 24 hours.

Reply: "in"
```

## Weak example (rejected by the skill)

```
I'm excited to share with you a great opportunity!

A client got results with podcast pitching!

If you've been thinking about being on podcasts, you should check
this out. We're opening doors and we don't want you to miss out!

Click here to learn more. Or book a call. Or reply with questions.
```

This fails on:
- Banned RRO ("excited to share", "opening doors", "don't miss out")
- No proof density (lines 4-6 are vague — no names, dates, numbers)
- Three CTAs instead of one

## Subject line guidance

Strong subject lines for the 12-line format follow one of three patterns:
- **Specific result**: "2 interviews from 1 post (Tracy's Tuesday)"
- **Named skeptic question**: "You said podcasts are too slow"
- **Completed action**: "I just closed 4 of the 6 spots"

Avoid: "Quick question", "Checking in", "You might like this", "Exclusive offer inside"
