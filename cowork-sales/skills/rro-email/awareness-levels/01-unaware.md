# Awareness Level 1: Unaware

## Who they are
They don't know they have a problem — or they've normalized it so completely they no longer feel the pain. They are not actively looking for solutions.

## What they know
Nothing about your category, your offer, or the gap in their situation. If you open with a product pitch they will ignore it or mark it spam.

## Lead with
A surprising story or stat — something that creates a crack in their worldview. They need to see the problem before they can care about the solution. Lead with your world, not theirs.

## Sample opener
```
Last month I lost $40K from a single mistake I'd been making for two years.
The worst part: I didn't even know it was a mistake.
```

Or:

```
A study released in April tracked 400 service businesses over 18 months.
The ones that grew more than 30% all had one thing in common — and it's
not what the productivity crowd is selling.
```

## CTA strength
**Soft.** Ask for a reply, a click to read more, or a survey response. Never lead with Buy or Apply. The ask should feel like curiosity, not commitment.

## Subject line approach
- Mysterious, story-driven: "the mistake I didn't know I was making"
- Stat-led: "400 businesses, 18 months, one common thread"
- Confessional: "I was wrong about this for 4 years"

Never: product names, benefit promises, or urgency language at this stage.

## When to use this calibration
- Cold email to a purchased or rented list
- Ad-driven cold traffic entering an email sequence for the first time
- A re-engagement campaign for subscribers who've been dormant 90+ days
- Anyone who found you through organic content and hasn't bought before
