# Awareness Level 3: Solution-Aware

## Who they are
They know solutions to their problem exist. They may have tried some. They are actively comparing approaches — but they don't know your specific method or offer yet.

## What they know
The category of solution. They may know your competitors. They have opinions about what hasn't worked. They are skeptical of generic promises.

## Lead with
Differentiate your approach. Don't sell the outcome — they know the outcome is possible. Sell why your method works differently, and why the approaches they've already tried failed.

## Sample opener
```
Every productivity system tells you to batch your tasks into time blocks.
That advice works for solo operators. For service business owners managing
client deliverables across 6 accounts, it makes things worse.
Here's the actual problem with time-blocking and what I use instead.
```

Or:

```
You've probably read the same 3 books on pricing that everyone recommends.
They're written for product businesses. If you're a service provider,
the model is different — and the chapter they always skip is the one
that actually matters.
```

## CTA strength
**Medium.** They're ready to learn more. A link to a detailed free resource, a case study, or a low-ticket offer fits. A full-price direct buy is appropriate if proof density is high enough in the email body.

## Subject line approach
- Contrast: "why [popular method] doesn't work for [specific audience]"
- Reframe: "the problem with every [category] course is the same thing"
- Specific differentiation: "what [your approach] does that [common approach] misses"

## When to use this calibration
- Mid-funnel email subscribers (60-120 days on list, multiple opens)
- People who've attended a free training but haven't bought
- Referral leads who come in already knowing about your category
- Anyone who's mentioned a competitor to you or in a survey
