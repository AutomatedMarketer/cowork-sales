# Awareness Level 4: Product-Aware

## Who they are
They know your offer. They've been on your list, seen your content, maybe looked at the sales page. They haven't bought. There's a specific reason — and your job is to name it and address it directly.

## What they know
Your offer exists. They have a real objection — price, timing, skepticism about the result for their specific situation, or a competing priority. Generic "here's what's inside" emails won't move them.

## Lead with
Address the specific objection. Don't re-pitch the offer. Acknowledge that they know it exists. Name the most likely reason they haven't acted and answer it with proof, not reassurance.

## Sample opener
```
I know you've been on my list for 6 months and haven't signed up.
The most common reason people give me is "I don't have time right now."
Here's what I actually hear when I ask what that means:
they're afraid to start something they won't finish.
That's a different problem — and it's the one the program solves first.
```

Or:

```
If you've looked at the Momentum Method page more than once and haven't
enrolled, I want to tell you one thing:

The person I built it for is specifically the one who's looked at a lot
of programs and hasn't found one that fits their actual situation.

The first module is a single 30-minute call. That's the whole commitment
before you decide anything.
```

## CTA strength
**Direct.** They know the offer. The buy link belongs in this email. Remove friction: restate the price, state exactly what happens next after they click, and name the guarantee clearly.

## Subject line approach
- Direct objection: "the reason most people don't enroll (and whether it applies to you)"
- Specific acknowledgment: "you've been on my list for [time] — here's why I think you haven't bought"
- Proof against the specific blocker: "the '30-minute start' for people who are afraid to commit"

## When to use this calibration
- Subscribers who've clicked the sales page link but haven't purchased
- Cart abandoners
- People who've replied asking questions but haven't pulled the trigger
- Your highest-engagement email openers who've never bought anything
- Anyone who said "I'm interested, maybe later" more than 30 days ago
