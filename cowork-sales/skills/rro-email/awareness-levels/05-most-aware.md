# Awareness Level 5: Most-Aware

## Who they are
They want your offer. They've decided. They're waiting for a specific reason to act today — a deadline, a final reminder, a nudge that makes "right now" feel more obvious than "later."

## What they know
Everything. Your story, your proof, your offer details, your price. What they lack is the final activation signal. Your email's only job is to provide that signal clearly and without noise.

## Lead with
A direct CTA plus a real urgency trigger. No story, no warm-up, no re-pitching. State the deadline, state what they get, and get out of the way.

## Sample opener
```
You said yes 3 weeks ago. This is the final reminder before I close the
doors Friday at 5pm EST.
```

Or:

```
The cohort starts Monday. 6 of the 8 spots are filled.
If you're still thinking about it, this is the email.
```

## CTA strength
**Maximum.** One link. One action. The buy button. Pair with a hard deadline that is real — a cohort start date, a capacity constraint, a price change. Never manufacture urgency at this level; they'll see through it instantly because they've been watching you.

## Subject line approach
- Countdown: "Friday 5pm — doors close"
- Acknowledgment: "you've been thinking about this long enough"
- Specificity: "2 spots left — cohort starts Monday"

Never: re-explain the offer, add new benefits, or soften the close. They know. Say the thing.

## The one rule for Level 5 copy

Every sentence that isn't the CTA or the deadline is a reason for them to delay. Cut to minimum. The email should feel like a calendar alert, not a sales letter.

## When to use this calibration
- Existing buyers being offered an upsell or renewal
- Your "Top 100" hand-picked list — people you know are ready
- Launch close emails (Day 5 of a 5-day sequence)
- Anyone who has asked "when are you opening this again?" — they are Level 5 from the moment the offer reopens
- People currently in a trial or free tier who haven't upgraded
